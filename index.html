Website Week 2 Week 3

1. What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

Richard Meier's website is unique in that it requires viewers to adjust their browser size right at the start. A normally wide webpage does not directly begin the interactive experience; it must be switched to a long, vertical interface. During this resizing process, the scene in the display changes, with the sunset gradually rising to a midday feeling, and the light and shadow changing accordingly. This is the first action the website forces the viewer to undertake.

2. Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

1. Adjust the webpage size to the appropriate starting interface, and observe the changes in the display.

2. Click the start button, and music begins to play.

3. Read two segments of text, press the next button.

4. The display then transitions to a 3D scene, or rather, it uses animation to create the effect of a 3D scene, which can be explored by dragging with the mouse.

5. There is a wheel at the bottom of the webpage, which, by dragging, can move to the next scene or return to the previous one.

6. Each scene is 3D and can be observed by dragging; the third scene requires clicking on a glowing object to interact and trigger an animation to continue.

7. Some scenes play a video when a button is pressed, and some have a prompt to increase the volume.

8. Continuously turn the dial to switch to the next scene and read the story.

3. What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

The aspect where I spent the most time during this experience was using the mouse to drag the display, exploring the entire scene and story.

4. What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

The most common actions in these two minutes were dragging the display and turning the wheel.

5. What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

The main goal of this interactive experience is to present a story through an immersive experience by letting the audience drag the display to watch the plot.

6. How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

The website initially asking viewers to adjust the webpage size reduces the viewer's perspective, forcing them to drag the mouse to explore the entire scene, thus better understanding the content and atmosphere of the story conveyed.

7. What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

The most common actions in these two minutes were dragging the display and turning the wheel.

8. How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

As time progresses, viewers can see different scenes and stories, and by interacting with the scenes, they can better feel the story and atmosphere.

9. What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

This experience utilizes digital animation to convey information.

10. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

This reference conveyed how to enhance their interest in continuing to read and explore a long story through interaction, and by using interesting visual transformations to force viewers to minimize their browser, reducing their view and increasing the amount to see by dragging.

11. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

This reference uses music and atmospheric design in animations to provide a more immersive experience, allowing viewers to feel the atmosphere and emotions conveyed by the story. The pace of interaction and animation is slow, allowing one to gradually enjoy a relaxing atmosphere.

12. What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

1. Initially resizing the browser to make it smaller is not possible with all browsers; it works with Chrome but not Safari.

2. After minimizing the display, the visible area becomes very small, which may cause viewers to lose interest in continuing to watch.

3. The story is very long and takes a lot of time to view the entire interaction.

13. What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

1. The overall design and style of the website are very beautiful.

2. AThe music is very comfortable, allowing me to keep it running even if I'm not looking at the website.

3. Dragging the mouse to explore the scenes is very enjoyable and visually pleasing.
